Why it matters: A 3-node cluster can serve many more jobs when small tasks are packed onto shared nodes, reserving dedicated capacity only for large workloads.

💡 CONCEPT: Cache Invalidation
What it is: The process of marking cached data as stale and triggering a refresh — famously called “one of the two hard problems in computer science.”
Our approach: Three layers of protection — time-based TTL, manual override, and automatic integrity checks. If any layer detects staleness, a full re-sync is triggered.
💡 CONCEPT: Data Integrity Checks
What it is: SHA-256 checksums computed at sync time and verified before every cache read. Guarantees data has not been corrupted in storage.
Why it matters: Compliance teams need proof that cached data matches the source. Checksum verification provides cryptographic certainty — not just “it looks right.”

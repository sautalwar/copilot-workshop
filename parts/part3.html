💡 Q&A — Cost & Pricing

The questions your finance team WILL ask — with prepared answers

Q: What is the total cost of running AKS runners vs GitHub-hosted?
GitHub-Hosted: $0.008/min × 15 min = $0.12 compute + $0.12 egress + $0.60 for 8-core = $0.84/run. AKS: $73/mo cluster (D4s v3) ÷ 1,000 runs = $0.073 + $0.00 egress + Azure Files = ~$0.065/run. That is a 92% reduction per run.
Q: What are the hidden costs we should know about?
Be transparent: AKS control plane ($0 for Standard tier), Azure Files storage (~$5/mo for 50GB cache), Log Analytics (~$2/mo for basic monitoring), and AKS node pool VMs ($73/mo for D4s v3). Total baseline: ~$80/month before any runs. This is the floor cost even if no workflows execute.
Q: Can we use Reserved Instances or Savings Plans to reduce AKS costs further?
Yes. Azure Reserved VM Instances (1-year or 3-year) save 30–60% on the node pool VMs. A 3-year reservation on D4s v3 drops the node cost from $73/mo to ~$30/mo. Combined with spot VMs for burst capacity, you can reduce the floor cost by 50%+.
Q: What is the break-even point? How many runs before AKS pays for itself?
At $80/mo base cost and $0.775 savings per run: break-even = 104 runs/month (about 5 runs/day). Most teams exceed this within the first week. At 1,000 runs/month, you save $695/mo net — the cluster pays for itself 8.7x over.
Q: What about GitHub’s Larger Runners? Don’t they solve the performance problem?
Larger runners give you more CPU/RAM (up to 64-core), but they are more expensive ($0.064/min for 8-core) and still have no persistent cache and full egress costs. For data-heavy workflows, AKS is 5–10x cheaper than larger runners because you eliminate egress and cache rebuilds.
Q: How do costs scale with 100GB+ datasets?
At 100GB, AWS egress alone is $9.00/run on GitHub-hosted. On AKS with Azure Files cache, egress drops to $0 after the first run (data is already local). The savings jump from 92% to 96%+. Annual savings for 50 workflows: $24,000+. The bigger your data, the bigger your savings.
Q: Can we use Spot VMs for further savings on AKS?
Yes, with a caveat. Azure Spot VMs save 60–90% on compute but can be evicted with 30s notice. Use them for: non-critical CI (linting, tests). Do NOT use them for: production deployments, jobs over 2 hours. Configure a mixed node pool: regular VMs for guaranteed capacity + spot VMs for burst. This cuts costs another 40% on average.
Q: What is the operational cost of managing AKS itself?
Honest answer: Plan for 4–8 hours/month of Kubernetes operations (upgrades, monitoring, troubleshooting). At $100/hr, that is $400–800/month. BUT: if your savings are $9,300/year ($775/month), the net ROI is still strongly positive. And most of the ops work can be automated with AKS auto-upgrade and node auto-repair.
